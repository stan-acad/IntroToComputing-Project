Dekada 70: Philippine Martial Law

On September 23, 1972, Ferdinand Marcos spoke to the nation by television and by radio to make it known that he had declared Martial Law in the Philippines.

Oplan Sagittarius : Prelude to Martial Law

A week before the actual declaration of Martial Law, a number of people had already received information that Marcos had drawn up a plan to completely take over the government and gain absolute rule. Senator Benigno S. Aquino Jr., during a September 13, 1972 privilege speech, exposed what was known as "Oplan Sagittarius" the senator said he had received a top-secret military plan given by Marcos himself to place Metro Manila and outlying areas under the control of the Philippine Constabulary as a prelude to Martial Law. Marcos was going to use a series of bombings in Metro Manila, including the 1971 Plaza Miranda bombing, as a justification for his takeover and subsequent authoritarian rule.

Under Martial Law.

Instead of its promised stability and prosperity, the imposition of Martial Law generated intense social conflicts and a worsening economic crisis. The nine-year military rule ordered by then President Ferdinand Marcos in 1972 unleashed a wave of crimes under international law and grave human rights violations, including tens of thousands of people arbitrarily arrested and detained, and thousands of others tortured, forcibly disappeared, and killed. Extensive human rights violations showed a pattern of widespread arrests and detention, enforced disappearances, killings and torture of people that were critical of the government or perceived as political opponents.

The Revolution. The Movement. The People Power.

Several factors contributed to the downfall of Philippine Martial Law, this includes economic mismanagement, human rights abuses, political repression, and especially growing opposition. The regime's corruption and crony capitalism led to economic decline, while widespread human rights violations and suppression of political dissent fueled public discontent. The assassination of opposition leader Benigno Aquino Jr. in 1983 intensified opposition, and the rigged snap elections in 1986 triggered the People Power Revolution.
